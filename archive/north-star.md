## Objective

Land 15 paying CRE clients on annual seat licenses within the next 90 days, on the path to $500K ARR by end of 2026. Drift AI delivers agentic AI to commercial real estate professionals — void analysis, lease abstraction, and the workflows they actually run — on the Hermes agentic layer, powered by the Dante LLM with citation-grounded, anti-hallucination answers from a sandboxed Vault of the client's own data, with GIS built in. Winning in this cycle means signed annual contracts with real CRE firms actively running these workflows in production — not pilots that stall, not architecture demos. Every dollar of effort this quarter should move a prospect toward a signed seat or keep a signed client live.

## Operating Rules

The brief cites these directly when flagging anti-priorities. Each is concrete enough that a queued task can clearly violate one.

- Software seats only — do not sell hours or bespoke consulting. Revenue comes from the product, not custom services.
- Annual contracts only — no monthly self-serve at our price point.
- No new Hermes/Dante capability unless a paying (or in-pipeline) CRE client asked for it this quarter. Do not build ahead of the revenue loop.
- Dante never answers without a Vault citation. Do not ship anything that weakens the anti-hallucination guardrail.
- Lead with CRE business outcomes (void analysis, lease abstraction), not the agentic/LLM architecture. Sell the result, not the Hermes/Dante internals.
- Protect margin: clients bring their own data into the Vault — no managed data-ops or hosting their datasets for them.

## Commercial Ladder

Pilot / POC → Single-team license → Firm-wide license → Enterprise (multi-team / multi-office rollout)

## Notes

Drift AI's defensible surface: Hermes (agentic orchestration layer), Dante (citation-grounded LLM that refuses to hallucinate), the Vault (client-owned-data sandbox that pulls data on demand rather than warehousing it), and built-in GIS. Positioning sells CRE outcomes; the architecture is the moat, not the pitch.
