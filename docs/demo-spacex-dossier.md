## 1. Executive summary

- **SpaceX is now public.** IPO priced **June 12, 2026 at $135/share**, raising ~**$75B** across ~555.6M shares. It closed its first day at **$161 (+19%)**.
- **Current price ~$163** (June 29–30, 2026), market cap **~$2.1–2.2 trillion** — making it one of the most valuable companies on earth. Wild first-two-weeks volatility: all-time high **$225.64** (Jun 16) → low **$147.11** (Jun 23).
- **The valuation re-rate was breathtaking and fast:** ~$350B (mid-2025) → $400B (Jul 2025) → **$800B secondary** (Dec 2025) → **$1.25T** via the **xAI merger (Feb 2026)** → **$1.75–2T IPO target** → ~$2.1T public cap. A ~6x in roughly a year.
- **The business that justifies it is Starlink.** Starlink is now the **majority of revenue (~61%)** and the **only meaningfully profitable segment** — ~$11.4B 2025 revenue (+~50% YoY), ~63% EBITDA margin, **10.3M subscribers** (Mar 2026) across 155 countries.
- **The risk is the price, not the company.** At ~96x trailing revenue, perfection is priced in. Negative free cash flow (**−$13.8B in 2025**), ARPU erosion, Amazon Kuiper competition, and Starship execution risk are the real bear case.
- **Analyst tone is constructive but cautious:** ~$187.80 average 12-month target (6 buy / 1 sell), i.e. modest upside from here with high dispersion.

**One-line thesis:** *A genuinely dominant, Starlink-driven cash engine wrapped in a moonshot (Starship + space AI), trading at a valuation that already assumes the moonshot works. The bear case isn't failure — it's "succeeds slower and more expensively than the price implies."*

## 3. Business fundamentals

### 3a. Starlink — the cash machine (the actual investment thesis)
- **Revenue:** ~$11.4B in 2025 (+~50% YoY) = **~61% of SpaceX's $18.7B total**. Q1 2026: **$3.26B revenue, $1.19B operating income**. 2026 run-rate ~**$15.5B**.
- **Subscribers:** 2.3M (2023) → 4.4M (2024) → 8.9M (2025) → **10.3M (Mar 2026)**, 155 countries. Quilty Space projects ~16.8M by end-2026.
- **Margins:** ~**63% EBITDA** — "looks like a software company"; ~$4.42B 2025 segment operating income, $7.17B adjusted profit (+86% YoY). The constellation is largely sunk cost; each new subscriber is near-zero marginal cost.
- **The crack in the story — ARPU erosion:** blended ARPU fell to **~$66/mo (Q1 2026)** from $86 a year earlier and $99 in 2023, as growth shifts to price-sensitive markets (Africa, SE Asia, LatAm). Volume up, price down — the math gets harder.

### 3b. Launch — strategic moat, weak P&L
- Rocket launches reportedly **lose money on a standalone basis**; their value is the moat (cadence, reusability, near-monopoly on heavy launch) and enabling Starlink deployment — not segment profit.

### 3c. Starship — the optionality (and the risk)
- **Flight 12 (May 22, 2026)** = first **V3** test (Raptor 3, 408 ft). Successful 33-engine ascent + deployed 20 Starlink simulators + 2 modified V3 sats, but **booster broke apart** on an off-nominal flip.
- SpaceX guides to **payload-to-orbit in H2 2026**; in-space propellant transfer + long-duration tests targeted for 2026. ~80–100+ SpaceX missions planned in 2026.
- **Why it matters financially:** much of the >$1T premium rests on Starship reaching commercial viability (cheap mass-to-orbit → Starlink V3, space data centers, lunar base). Analysts assign relatively **low probabilities to full near-term success** — high payoff, uncertain timing.

## 4. Catalysts vs. risks

**Catalysts (bull)**
- Starlink subscriber + revenue compounding (path toward ~$15.5B 2026), direct-to-cell, enterprise/maritime/aviation, government & defense contracts.
- Starship reaching orbital payload delivery in H2 2026 → unlocks next-gen Starlink + new markets.
- xAI/"AI in space" narrative + data-center ambitions feeding a premium multiple.
- Index inclusion / institutional flows now that it's public and ~$2T.

**Risks (bear)**
- **Valuation:** ~96x trailing revenue; "priced for perfection." Bear scenarios model a drop toward ~$75 (still ~$1T cap, ~60x sales).
- **Cash burn:** FCF **−$13.8B (2025)**, **−$9.1B (Q1 2026)** — may need external capital; public-market investors prize stability.
- **Competition:** **Amazon Kuiper** (approved, deploying, deep pockets), OneWeb, national broadband programs → pressure on Starlink pricing power.
- **ARPU compression** as noted.
- **Execution/technical:** Starship failures, regulatory (FAA/environmental), launch mishaps.
- **Key-person/governance:** Musk concentration; merged xAI structure adds complexity and correlation to AI sentiment.
- **Volatility:** $147–$226 range in two weeks signals an unstable price-discovery phase.

## 5. How to get exposure now (post-IPO)

- **Simplest:** buy **SPCX** directly on Nasdaq through any broker (Robinhood, Fidelity, Schwab, IBKR, etc.). No accreditation needed anymore.
- **Pre-IPO vehicles are now mostly legacy/redundant** but still exist as indirect exposure: **Destiny Tech100 (DXYZ)** (~12% SpaceX historically), **ARK Venture (ARKVX)**. These may trade at premiums/discounts to NAV and now overlap with simply holding SPCX.
- **Secondary marketplaces** (Forge, Hiive, EquityZen) were the pre-IPO path ($20K–$100K mins, accredited only) — largely moot post-IPO.
- **Practical note:** given two-week volatility, mechanics matter — consider limit orders, position sizing, and awareness of any post-IPO **lockup expiry** (insider selling can pressure price; confirm the lockup date from the prospectus). *Not financial advice.*
